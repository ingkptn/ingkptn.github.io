(in)tangible is an fictional exhibition showcasing work by 6 ceramics deemed as Japan's National Living Treasures. Components include a printed exhibition catalog, posters, merchandise, website landing page design, and more.
*Note: this project was completed as part of CD Studio: Multiple Editions, Fall 2023, at the Parsons School of Design.

1. Identity

The general idea for this typeface originally stemmed from my fascination with industrial aesthetics, as well as my tendency to use monspaced typefaces in my projects. After researching existing typefaces, a rough sketch of the characters was drawn and digitized.

2. Type Specimen

Since the whole idea of my typeface was the concept of building and constructing, I wanted it to be more interactive and modular, and so I came up with the idea of making a stack of cards. Although there is an order to the cards, they can still be moved around. When laid out in order, the back of the cards form a "poster" showcasing some characters in 3D form.
