in praise of shadows

interaction / web design

Storytelling website centered around an excerpt of "陰翳礼讃 / In Praise of Shadows" by Jun-Ichiro Tanizaki, a 1933 esay on Japanese aesthetics. The excerpt talks about light and shadows in Japanese architecture, and the website attempts to convey this as an interactive reading experience.

nyc in data: elevators in nyc

web design / data visualization

Data Visualization of the elevators in the different boroughs of NYC, taking into account data on capacity, speed, etc. Dataset was taken from https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/new-york-city and anomalies were filtered out so that only consistent data remains. After sorting dataset into JSON file, P5.js was used to create the visualization.

looking in

web design

"Looking In" is a collection of images and videos taken between 18:00 and 06:00 in New York city. Each image looks into a space through a frame, whether it be windows, doors, holes, etc. The collection as a whole challenges the concept of privacy, and explores ideas of invasion and intrusion. Data was sorted into a JSON file.
