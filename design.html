A Memento of Home


A book for Core 2: Typography exploring my notions of home, from a physical place to things beyond that. The content ranges from childhood memories to traditional beliefs and values, and the simple design system reflects the style I am reminded of when thinking of the word "home".

Periodic Table Poster


Poster of the Periodic table of Elements emphasizing grid systems and typographic hierarchy. My design was made to resemble black-and-white photocopied worksheets we had back in middle school, and only one typeface was used.

Exhibition


Mock-ups of promotional material for an imagined exhibtiion for graffiti artist Alex Face, whose work surrounds a character of a child in a bunny costume. Both static and dynamic material were designed.

Favorit Type Specimen


32-page Type Specimen for ABC Favorit by Dinamo Type Foundry, showcasing sizes, weights, glyphs, anatomy, etc. Image shows mock-up of booklet.

Stories as Networks : In praise of Shadows

Storytelling website centered around an excerpt of "陰翳礼讃 / In Praise of Shadows" by Jun-Ichiro Tanizaki, a 1933 esay on Japanese aesthetics. The excerpt talks about light and shadows in Japanese architecture, and the website attempts to convey this as an interactive reading experience. Each card on the home page leads to a different part of the story with different interactive elements, allowing users to explore freely.

Elastic Collection : Looking In

"Looking In" is a collection of images and videos taken between 18:00 and 06:00 in New York city. Each image looks into a space through a frame, whether it be windows, doors, holes, etc. The collection as a whole challenges the concept of privacy, and explores ideas of invasion and intrusion. Data was sorted into JSON file.

NYC in Data : Elevators in NYC

Data Visualization of the elevators in the different boroughs of NYC, taking into account data on capacity, speed, etc. Dataset was taken from https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/new-york-city/nyc-elevators and anomalies were filtered out so that only consistent data remains. After sorting dataset into JSON file, P5.js was used to create the visualization.
